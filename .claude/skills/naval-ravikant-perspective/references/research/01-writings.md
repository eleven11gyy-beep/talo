# 01 - Writings and Systematic Thought

Subject: Naval Ravikant
Mode: public web research
Research date: 2026-05-09

## Primary sources

1. `nav.al/rich` - "How to Get Rich" full transcript. Confidence: high, first-party transcript.
   - Core framework: wealth is assets earning while you sleep; money is a transfer mechanism; status is zero-sum.
   - Repeated cluster: specific knowledge + accountability + leverage + judgment.
   - Emphasis: code and media are permissionless leverage; labor and capital require permission.
   - He treats incentive design as central and uses principal-agent logic to explain founder mentality.

2. `nav.al/specific-knowledge` - "Arm Yourself With Specific Knowledge." Confidence: high, first-party transcript.
   - Specific knowledge is discovered through curiosity, obsession, talent, and action.
   - It sits at the edge of knowledge and resists automation because it is tightly bound to the individual.

3. `nav.al/judgment` - "Judgment Is the Decisive Skill." Confidence: high, first-party transcript.
   - In an age of nearly infinite leverage, judgment becomes the scarce bottleneck.
   - Bad judgment is amplified by leverage; good judgment compounds through fewer, better decisions.

4. `nav.al/happiness`, `nav.al/desire`, `nav.al/skill` - happiness series. Confidence: high, first-party transcripts.
   - Happiness is a trainable skill, not merely a fixed trait.
   - Desire creates disturbance; peace improves judgment.
   - He connects inner state to practical effectiveness rather than treating philosophy as abstract.

5. `nav.al/find`, `nav.al/indirect` - 2025 short episodes. Confidence: high, first-party transcripts.
   - Latest reinforcement: specific knowledge is found through action; difficult competitive goals are often achieved indirectly.
   - Shows continuity rather than major model change.

## Curated source

1. `navalmanack.com/home` - The Almanack of Naval Ravikant. Confidence: medium-high, curated with Naval's permission but edited by Eric Jorgenson.
   - Collects tweets, essays, podcasts, and interviews over a decade.
   - Useful for cross-domain recurrence: wealth, judgment, happiness, self-inquiry.

2. `navalmanack.com/navals-recommended-reading` - recommended reading, updated 2024. Confidence: medium-high.
   - Intellectual lineage: Taleb, Munger, Feynman, David Deutsch, Karl Popper, Matt Ridley, Krishnamurti, Marcus Aurelius, Kapil Gupta, Jed McKenna.
   - Strong bias toward first principles, evolutionary explanations, physics-style clarity, epistemology, and direct self-observation.

## Recurring core claims

- Wealth is ownership, not hourly labor.
- Escape competition through authenticity; if you are competing head-on, you are already in the wrong game.
- Leverage makes small differences in judgment enormous.
- Reading, thinking, and judgment matter more than performative busyness.
- Happiness and peace are not luxuries; they are input conditions for good judgment.

## Notes for synthesis

- "Leverage" passes cross-domain recurrence: wealth, media/code, personal decisions, learning, and influence.
- "Specific knowledge" is not a skill list; it is identity-aligned knowledge discovered by doing.
- Naval's writing style is aphoristic, compressed, abstract-first, then illustrated with simple examples.
