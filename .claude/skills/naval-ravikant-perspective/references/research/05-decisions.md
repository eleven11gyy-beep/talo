# 05 - Decisions and Actions

Subject: Naval Ravikant
Research date: 2026-05-09

## Career and product decisions

1. Epinions -> startup/operator background.
   - Public bios identify Epinions as an early company he founded.
   - Relevance: later suspicion of principal-agent problems and emphasis on ownership likely came from operator/investor experience.
   - Confidence: medium-high from public bios.

2. Venture Hacks -> AngelList.
   - Pattern: turn private startup financing knowledge into public media, then into a marketplace/infrastructure company.
   - This is "productize yourself" before the phrase became popular: knowledge + media leverage + software + network effects.
   - Confidence: high from public company history and bios.

3. Angel investing in high-upside networks.
   - Known investments include Twitter, Uber, Notion, Yammer, Postmates, and many more according to public bios and profiles.
   - Relevance: reflects power-law, long-term game, and network/platform bias.
   - Confidence: high for examples named by Milken/Almanack; medium for exact counts.

4. Spearhead / Ravikant Capital.
   - Milken bio says he manages Ravikant Capital and Spearhead fund of funds.
   - Relevance: meta-leverage: backing founders who can themselves invest.
   - Confidence: high for bio, medium for operational detail.

5. Airchat.
   - TechCrunch reports a rebuilt/relaunched Airchat in April 2024, led by Naval and Brian Norgard.
   - Relevance: thesis that voice carries more human context than text; social product as an experiment in media and communication leverage.
   - Confidence: high for launch report.

## Decision logic visible in actions

- Build platforms rather than one-off services.
- Prefer permissionless leverage: writing, software, marketplace infrastructure.
- Attach reputation to a public name.
- Seek long-term games with compounding trust.
- Treat product and investing as experiments in incentives and networks.

## Tensions

- He criticizes status games yet operates in high-status Silicon Valley networks.
- He recommends peace and low desire while building/investing in highly ambitious companies.
- He warns against renting time, but the path to ownership may be less accessible outside tech/media/software markets.
